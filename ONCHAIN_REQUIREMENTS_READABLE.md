# Web2–Web3 Identity Verification Requirements (Human-Readable)

## Purpose
- The system shall provide trust-minimized verification of Web2 attributes.
- The system shall preserve user privacy when binding Web2 identity to Web3 addresses.
- The system shall securely store verification results.
- The system shall prevent impersonation and replay attacks.
- The system shall correctly map proofs to providers and criteria sets.
## Binding Requirements
- A Web3 user shall be able to bind to different Web2 identities per `providerId`.
  - Binding multiplicity shall follow provider-specific policy.
  - For `Binance`, one Web3 user shall have only one active bound nullifier at a time.
  - For providers other than `Binance` (including `GitHub`), one Web3 user may have multiple active bound nullifiers, subject to nullifier uniqueness constraints.
- The system shall support unbind operations.
- Binding shall be represented by storing `identityNullifier` and `user`.
- A Web2 identity (via `identityNullifier`) shall bind to only one Web3 user.
- Binding shall be enforced per provider (not per criteria).
  - `identityNullifier` shall be derived from `(providerId, providerUserId)`; therefore provider-scoped enforcement is correct by construction.
- After unbind, all historical data for that nullifier shall be wiped.
- Unbind shall be blocked if a cooldown window since the last bind or last result submission has not elapsed.
- Unbind shall be blocked if any downstream system has locked the result.
- Binding shall only occur through successful on-chain verification result submission; no manual bind operation shall exist.
- When a verification result is submitted on-chain and all other requirements are met, the system shall automatically bind the `identityNullifier` to `proofUser` if it is not already bound.
- The system shall provide a contract call to notify that an `identityNullifier` is in use or no longer in use by downstream services.
- The downstream usage notification call shall be restricted to whitelisted callers.
- The contract shall treat `inUse` as a downstream-defined lock signal that prevents unbind; the on-chain system does not interpret the business meaning beyond lock/unlock.
- The system shall allow multiple downstream services to use the same verification result concurrently.
- An `identityNullifier` shall be considered locked if any whitelisted downstream service reports `inUse = true` for that nullifier.
- A verification result shall be reusable by multiple downstream services without regenerating proofs.
- Downstream locks shall not use TTL-based auto-expiry; unlock shall be explicit by the locking service.
- The system shall provide a governed deadlock-resolution path for exceptional cases where downstream locks are not released.
## Security Requirements
- Proofs shall be user-bound by including `proofUser` in the zkVM output.
- Verification results shall be stored under `proofUser` regardless of transaction sender.
- The system shall reject submissions where `identityNullifier` is already bound to another user.
- The system shall validate `providerCriteriaId` (on-chain or off-chain registry).
- Replay enforcement shall use `timestamp` and `nonce` as replay-prevention fields.
- The contract shall reject reused timestamps and reused nonces within replay domain scope.
  - `proofHash` is not required for replay protection.
- The contract shall reject proofs older than the latest stored result for the same user and criteria.
- The system shall allow relayer-submitted transactions while still enforcing that results are stored under `proofUser`.
- There shall be no manual bind operation; binding shall only occur via successful verification result submission.
- Downstream lock/unlock notifications shall be accepted only from whitelisted callers to prevent malicious locking or unlocking of bindings.
- The contract shall define a canonical replay domain `ReplayDomain`.
- `ReplayDomain` shall include at least `chainId`, `contractAddress`, `proofUser`, `identityNullifier`, and `providerCriteriaId`.
- For proxy-based deployments, `contractAddress` in `ReplayDomain` shall be the proxy address (`address(this)` at execution).
- Nonce replay checks shall use `ReplayDomain` and reject reuse only within the same `ReplayDomain`.
- Timestamp replay checks shall use `ReplayDomain` and reject replay only within the same `ReplayDomain`.
## Verification Result Requirements
- Each stored result shall include: `providerCriteriaId`, `identityNullifier`, `providerOutput`, and any extra required fields.
- Users may have multiple results per `providerCriteriaId`.
- Only the latest result per `(user, providerCriteriaId)` shall be active.
- Multi-criteria proofs are supported; their combined output shall be stored as an opaque blob, and downstream services are responsible for interpretation and usage.
- The on-chain contract treats `providerOutput` as opaque bytes even though zkVM defines explicit encoding/decoding for downstream interpretation.
- The system shall always store the full `providerOutput` blob on-chain for downstream business-logic usage.
- The system shall enforce maximum `providerOutput` size limits to prevent calldata/storage abuse.
- The system shall support versioned/schema-prefixed `providerOutput` envelopes and reject malformed envelopes.
- Allowed `providerOutput` schema/version shall be bound to `providerCriteriaId`.
- Each verification result shall expose `timestamp` as part of the stored and queryable result data.
## Functional Requirements
- The system shall implement `unbindIdentity(user, identityNullifier)`.
- `unbindIdentity` shall fail if the cooldown window since last bind or last result submission has not elapsed.
- `unbindIdentity` shall fail if any downstream system has locked the result.
- `unbindIdentity` shall be callable only by `proofUser`.
- `unbindIdentity` shall wipe all historical data for the nullifier upon success.
  - The system shall implement `reportDownstreamUsage(identityNullifier, inUse)` to lock/unlock unbind based on downstream usage.
  - `reportDownstreamUsage` shall be callable only by whitelisted addresses.
  - `reportDownstreamUsage` shall only toggle the unbind lock state; any semantic meaning of `inUse` is defined by the downstream service.
  - For lock creation (`inUse = true`), the contract shall verify `identityNullifier` is actively bound.
  - The system shall track locks by `(service, identityNullifier)` and allow unlock only by the same service for that nullifier.
  - Unbind shall be blocked if any active downstream lock exists for the same `identityNullifier`.
  - User signature per downstream service shall not be required for lock creation.
  - Lock policy shall be `whitelisted service + bound identityNullifier`.
  - Repeated `inUse = true` calls from the same service for the same `identityNullifier` shall be idempotent.
  - The contract shall not apply timeout-based or TTL-based automatic unlock.
  - Unlock (`inUse = false`) shall validate lock ownership and lock existence for `identityNullifier` only.
  - On successful unbind, lock state for that `identityNullifier` shall be cleared together with binding/result data to prevent orphan locks.
  - Any privileged wipe or migration path that removes result/binding data shall also clear corresponding downstream lock state.
  - The system shall implement a governance-controlled emergency lock-resolution operation for exceptional deadlock cases.
  - Emergency lock resolution shall be restricted by governance authorization policy and shall require an explicit reason code.
  - Emergency lock resolution shall emit auditable events with caller, reason, affected `identityNullifier` lock, and timestamp.
  - The system shall expose lock-state observability (events and/or queries) sufficient to identify long-lived locks and operational deadlock risk.
  - Each service shall have at most one lock per `identityNullifier`.
- The system shall implement `submitVerificationResult(proof)`.
- `submitVerificationResult` shall verify the zkVM proof.
- `submitVerificationResult` shall validate `providerCriteriaId` if it is maintained on-chain.
- `submitVerificationResult` shall enforce impersonation resistance and replay protection.
- `submitVerificationResult` shall store results under `proofUser`.
- `submitVerificationResult` shall update the latest-result pointer per `(user, providerCriteriaId)`.
- `submitVerificationResult` shall fail if:
  - the `identityNullifier` is bound to another user, or
  - the `providerCriteriaId` is unregistered (if on-chain), or
  - the proof is stale or replayed, or
  - the proof is invalid.
- `submitVerificationResult` shall auto-bind `identityNullifier` to `proofUser` if no binding exists and all other requirements are met.
- The contract shall use deterministic, standardized error codes/messages for all rejection paths.
- The system shall implement whitelist governance functions: `addWhitelistedService`, `removeWhitelistedService`, and `setWhitelistedServiceStatus`.
- Whitelist governance actions shall be restricted to governance-authorized roles.
- Whitelisted service status shall support at least `ACTIVE`, `PAUSED`, and `REVOKED`.
- `reportDownstreamUsage` shall reject callers whose whitelist status is not `ACTIVE`.
- The governance framework shall support emergency revoke for compromised services.
- Whitelist governance actions shall emit auditable events with actor, target service, old/new status, reason, and timestamp.
- Revoked services shall not create new locks.
- Policy for existing locks from revoked services shall be governance-resolvable and auditable.
- `submitVerificationResult` shall validate `providerOutput` length against configured limits.
- `submitVerificationResult` shall validate `providerOutput` envelope structure, including prefix/version/schema and payload length consistency.
- `submitVerificationResult` shall enforce schema/version allowlist mapping for the given `providerCriteriaId`.
- Auto-binding on `submitVerificationResult` shall enforce provider-specific binding multiplicity policy for the corresponding provider.
## Query Requirements
- The system shall implement `getLatestResult(user, providerCriteriaId)`.
- `getLatestResult` shall return the latest result for the user and criteria.
- The system may implement `getAllResults(user, providerCriteriaId)`.
- If `getAllResults` is not implemented, event-based indexing shall be supported.
- The system shall provide at least one full-history retrieval path for `(user, providerCriteriaId)`: on-chain `getAllResults` or event-based indexing.
## Constraints and Invariants
- One Web2 identity shall map to only one Web3 user.
- No raw Web2 IDs shall be stored on-chain.
- Only `identityNullifier` shall be stored on-chain.
- `identityNullifier` shall be deterministic.
- `providerCriteriaId` shall be deterministic.
- zkVM outputs shall be deterministic.
- The system shall produce audit artifacts for external review, including logs and evidence of domain separation and replay protection checks.
## Non-Functional Requirements
- The system shall be gas-efficient.
- The system shall be upgradeable.
- The system shall use a modular design.
- The system shall define governance for provider and criteria changes, including approval authority and versioning to prevent fragmentation.
- The governance framework shall define deadlock-resolution policy, escalation workflow, and accountability for emergency lock actions.
- The governance framework shall define whitelist operational controls, including review cadence, escalation, and emergency response procedures.
## Performance Evaluation Matrix
- Metrics shall include gas cost per operation (`bind` via submit, `unbind`, `submitVerificationResult`, `getLatestResult`, and downstream usage reporting), storage growth per result, and query latency (on-chain view call and off-chain indexing).
- Measurements shall be reported for typical and worst-case inputs (including multi-criteria proofs and large `providerOutput` blobs).
- The report shall identify primary gas/storage cost drivers and optimization opportunities.
- The report shall compare results against at least one baseline (prior version or alternative implementation) and explain deltas.
## Standard Error Codes
- `ERR_NULLIFIER_BOUND`: `identityNullifier` is already bound to another user.
- `ERR_PROVIDER_CRITERIA_UNREGISTERED`: `providerCriteriaId` is not registered (when on-chain registry is used).
- `ERR_PROOF_REPLAY`: proof is stale, timestamp is older than latest, or timestamp/nonce is replayed within replay domain scope.
- `ERR_PROOF_INVALID`: zkVM proof verification failed.
- `ERR_UNBIND_COOLDOWN`: unbind cooldown window has not elapsed.
- `ERR_DOWNSTREAM_LOCKED`: downstream usage lock is active.
- `ERR_UNAUTHORIZED`: caller is not authorized (e.g., non-whitelisted or not `proofUser`).
- `ERR_INPUT_MALFORMED`: input data or required fields are missing or malformed.
- `ERR_NULLIFIER_NOT_BOUND`: `identityNullifier` is not actively bound.
- `ERR_DOWNSTREAM_UNLOCK_OWNER_MISMATCH`: unlock attempted by a different service owner for the same `identityNullifier`.
- `ERR_DOWNSTREAM_NOT_LOCKED`: unlock attempted when no owned active lock exists.
- `ERR_GOVERNANCE_REQUIRED`: operation requires governance authorization.
- `ERR_SERVICE_NOT_ACTIVE`: caller service is not in `ACTIVE` whitelist status.
- `ERR_PROVIDER_OUTPUT_TOO_LARGE`: `providerOutput` exceeds configured size limit.
- `ERR_PROVIDER_OUTPUT_MALFORMED`: `providerOutput` envelope or length fields are invalid.
- `ERR_PROVIDER_OUTPUT_SCHEMA_UNSUPPORTED`: `providerOutput` schema/version is not allowed for `providerCriteriaId`.
## Auditability Requirements
- The system shall emit events for downstream lock create/update/release operations with `service`, `identityNullifier`, and `inUse`.
- The system shall provide sufficient audit records to reconstruct active-lock state and lock ownership history per `identityNullifier`.
- Emergency lock-resolution actions shall emit dedicated events including governance actor, reason code, and affected `identityNullifier` lock.
- Whitelist governance actions shall emit dedicated events including actor, service, status transition, reason, and timestamp.
## Replay Scope Rationale
- Global nonce uniqueness is not required because unrelated users and criteria may generate proofs concurrently.
- Domain-scoped timestamp/nonce checks prevent false rejection across independent users/criteria while preserving replay protection within the same verification context.
